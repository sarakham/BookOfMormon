 Behold, we cannot understand the words which our father hath spoken concerning the natural branches of the olive tree, and also concerning the Gentiles. We have not; for the Lord maketh no such thing known unto us. What meaneth this thing which our father saw in a dream? What meaneth the tree which he saw? What meaneth the rod of iron which our father saw, that led to the tree? What meaneth the river of water which our father saw? Doth this thing mean the torment of the body in the days of probation, or doth it mean the final state of the soul after the death of the temporal body, or doth it speak of the things which are temporal? Thou hast declared unto us hard things, more than we are able to bear. Thou speakest hard things against us. Our brother is a fool, for he thinketh that he can build a ship; yea, and he also thinketh that he can cross these great waters.
